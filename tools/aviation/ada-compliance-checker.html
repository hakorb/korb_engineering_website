Recent Guidance & Updates

2024 DOJ Rule: Web & Mobile Accessibility (Title II)
Final rule published April 2024 requires state and local government web content and mobile apps to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Compliance dates: large entities (50,000+ pop.) by April 2026; smaller entities by April 2027.
EV Charging Station Accessibility (2023)
DOJ and DOT issued guidance clarifying that EV charging stations funded by federal programs must be accessible. Requirements include accessible route to chargers, operable parts within reach range, clear floor space, and accessible payment systems.
Outdoor Developed Areas
Access Board guidelines for trails, picnic areas, camping facilities, beach access routes, and viewing areas. Trail standards: 36" min tread width, max 1:12 slope for up to 200 ft segments, rest intervals, firm and stable surface.
Emergency Evacuation Requirements
Areas of rescue assistance required in buildings with exits not accessible. Visual alarms (strobes) in all common areas and restrooms. Evacuation chairs or platform lifts at stairwells. Audible and visible notification appliances.

Fair Housing Act (FHA) Comparison

FHA Accessibility (42 U.S.C. 3604) applies to multifamily dwellings with 4+ units (first occupied after March 13, 1991). Seven design requirements include: accessible building entrance on an accessible route, accessible common areas, usable doors, accessible route into/through dwelling, accessible environmental controls/switches/outlets, reinforced bathroom walls for grab bars, usable kitchens and bathrooms. FHA requirements are less extensive than ADA/ABA for public facilities but apply to housing that ADA typically does not cover.

State-Specific Notes

Important: Many states and municipalities adopt stricter accessibility requirements than the federal ADA Standards. Always check applicable state building codes. Notable examples:

• California (CBC Ch. 11B): Often stricter; e.g., 1:10 max flared-side slope at curb ramps, 48" min width accessible routes in some contexts
• Texas (TAS): Texas Accessibility Standards may differ on signage and parking requirements
• Florida (FBC Ch. 553): References ADA Standards with additional requirements for hurricane shelters and coastal facilities
• New York: NYC Building Code has additional accessibility provisions for Type B dwelling units
• Illinois (IAC): Illinois Accessibility Code has broader scope covering smaller buildings
