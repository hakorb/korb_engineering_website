AIP Primer

AIP (Airport Improvement Program) is FAA's grant-in-aid for public-use airports in the NPIAS. Funded from the Airport & Airway Trust Fund (passenger tickets, fuel taxes, cargo waybill tax).

Federal Share

75% at primary hubs (lg/med/sm), 90% at non-hub, non-primary, reliever, and GA airports. Certain safety and security projects at any airport may qualify for enhanced share.

  • Large/Med hub: 75%
  • Small hub: 75% (90% in some yrs)
  • Non-hub: 90%
  • Reliever / GA: 90%

Entitlement vs. Discretionary

Entitlement: formula-based — passenger enplanements, cargo landed weight, and the $150K non-primary entitlement (NPE).

Discretionary: Administrator allocates remaining funds by NPIAS priority, ACIP rank, and regional ADO recommendation.

BIL / FAA Reauth

Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (2021-2026) added $15B over 5 yrs as Airport Infrastructure Grants (AIG) at 100% federal for most primary airports — no local match required for qualifying projects.

FAA Reauthorization Act of 2024 extended AIP through FY 2028 at approximately $4B/yr.

PFC Interaction

Passenger Facility Charges ($4.50 max per enplanement) often fund the local share at primary airports. Collecting PFCs triggers reduced AIP passenger entitlement (50-75% turn-back to discretionary).

State Block Grant (SBGP)

10 states administer AIP for non-primary airports directly: GA, IL, MI, MN, MO, NC, NH, NJ, PA, TX, WI. Sponsors work with state DOT aviation division instead of FAA ADO.

Eligibility Basics

Airside, safety, environmental, and capacity work: eligible. Revenue-producing facilities (hangars, FBO buildings, terminal retail, parking garages): not AIP-eligible.

Grant Assurances

Accepting AIP obligates sponsor to 39 grant assurances (20-yr term; land acquisition = perpetuity) — rates & charges, self-sustaining, fee/rental consistency, etc.
