WHY AIRFIELD MARKINGS MATTER

Airfield markings are a pilot's primary visual guidance during ground operations. Unlike roadway markings, airport markings must comply with strict FAA dimensional standards so a pilot at 140 kts on final or taxiing a wide-body jet can identify runway ends, aiming points, touchdown zones, and hold lines unambiguously from a cockpit 40+ feet above the surface. Runway designation numbers are sized to be legible from pattern altitude. Threshold bars communicate runway width. Touchdown zone markings calibrate flare distance. Hold lines prevent runway incursions — a leading cause of aviation accidents.

Because markings drive safety, P-620 paint and glass beads must meet exact reflectivity and retroreflective requirements. Quantities and coverage rates are tightly specified and subject to inspection.

CALCULATION NOTES

  • Paint default: 115 SF/gal waterborne (AC 150/5370-10 P-620)
  • Glass beads: 6–8 lb/gal paint (Type I / Type III)
  • Primer (PCC surfaces): 120 SF/gal typical epoxy/alkyd
  • HMA density: 145 pcf default dense-graded
  • PCC volume: SF × in ÷ 324 = CY (+ waste factor)
  • Milling: SY-inch = (SF ÷ 9) × depth-in
  • Prime/tack: gal = (SF ÷ 9) × rate
