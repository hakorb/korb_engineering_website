Minimum Agent Quantities & Response 14 CFR 139.319

Response Time (139.319(h))
Within 3 minutes from the time of alarm, at least one required aircraft rescue and firefighting vehicle must reach the midpoint of the farthest runway serving air carrier operations from its assigned post (or reach any other specified point of comparable distance on the movement area) and begin application of extinguishing agent.
Subsequent Response (139.319(h)(2))
All other required vehicles must reach the same point no later than 4 minutes from the time of alarm and begin application of agent.
Discharge Rate (139.319(j))
Vehicles must discharge agent at rates not less than those specified for the quantity of water and aqueous film-forming foam (AFFF) tabulated in 139.317. Discharge must be controllable and capable of at least 50% of the rated flow.
On-Scene Availability (139.319(a))
Required ARFF vehicles, agents, and trained personnel must be available during all air carrier operations, including unscheduled operations after notification per 139.203.
Personnel (139.319(i))
Each vehicle must be operated by personnel trained per 139.319(i) — including initial and recurring training on aircraft familiarization, firefighting operations, rescue, hazmat, and live-fire drills (annual).
Reduction of Index (139.319(c))
When the longest aircraft has fewer than 5 avg daily departures, the certificate holder may provide a lower level of service — but never below Index A during air carrier operations.
