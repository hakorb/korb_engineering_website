SSD Equation

AASHTO uses d = 1.47Vt + V²/(30(a/g ± G)). Grade term reduces SSD on upgrades and increases on downgrades. Flat approximation: d = 1.47Vt + 1.075V²/a with t=2.5s, a=11.2 ft/s².

Vehicle Factor

Heavy trucks typically require ~1.40× passenger-car SSD due to longer braking distance; however, Green Book notes truck driver eye height (7.6 ft) partially offsets this — net SSD may be similar. Calculator applies a conservative 1.40× multiplier when "Truck" is selected.

Pavement

AASHTO 11.2 ft/s² deceleration corresponds to wet-pavement braking that 90% of drivers can achieve. Dry pavement condition in this tool applies a ~1.25× higher deceleration (14.0 ft/s²) producing a shorter SSD — not an AASHTO design value, but useful for investigation.
