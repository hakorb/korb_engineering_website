About This Tool

      
A one-screen planner for the day of a concrete pour. Inputs are the pour geometry, placement method, and crew setup. Outputs are the quantity to order, truck arrival interval, total duration, crew size, and a printable pour ticket.


      
ASTM C94 — 90-min / 300-rev Rule

      
ASTM C94 limits the time between batching and complete discharge to 90 minutes (or 300 revolutions of the drum at mixing speed), whichever comes first. The intent is to prevent hydration and workability loss before the concrete is placed. Exceeding the limit risks compromised strength, set behavior, and finishability.

      
When ambient temperatures exceed roughly 85°F, many specifications tighten the limit to 60 minutes. The TRUCKS tab flags any ticket whose estimated batch-to-placement exceeds the limit you select.


      
Hot & Cold Weather (ACI 305 / ACI 306)

      
    
        
  • ACI 305 — Hot-Weather Concreting. Typical triggers: air temp >85°F, concrete temp >90°F, high evaporation rate. Common mitigations: chilled/ice water, shade aggregate stockpiles, retarder, later pour start.
    • 
        
  • ACI 306 — Cold-Weather Concreting. Typical triggers: air temp <40°F or forecast <40°F within the protection period. Common mitigations: heated water, accelerator, blankets, enclosures; avoid heavy fly ash replacement.
    • 
      


      
Testing Frequency (ACI 318 §26.12 basis)

      
    
        
  • Slump (ASTM C143) — at first truck, then as needed if mix behavior changes.
    • 
        
  • Air content (ASTM C231 pressure, or C173 volumetric for lightweight).
    • 
        
  • Unit weight (ASTM C138).
    • 
        
  • Strength cylinders (ASTM C31) — per ACI 318, one sample per 150 cy (or fraction) of each mix, with a minimum of one sample per day per class. Many specifications require more (e.g., one set per 100 cy or fraction).
    • 
        
  • Temperature log at each truck (ambient and concrete).
    • 
      


      
Disclaimer

      
Placement rates, finish windows, and truck counts are planning estimates. Always coordinate with the batch plant, pump operator, and concrete finisher in advance of pour day. Cold- and hot-weather provisions may modify placement limits. Approximate mix proportions shown are indicative only and are not a substitute for a designed mix from a qualified mix designer / producer.
