Terzaghi general bearing-capacity equation:

qult = c · Nc · Sc · dc · ic + q · Nq · Sq · dq · iq + ½ · γ · B · Nγ · Sγ · dγ · iγ

Capacity factors (Meyerhof / Prandtl):

Nq = eπ tanφ · tan²(45 + φ/2)
Nc = (Nq − 1) / tanφ (φ>0); Nc = 5.14 (φ=0)
Nγ (Vesic) = 2(Nq + 1) tanφ
Nγ (Meyerhof) = (Nq − 1) tan(1.4φ)
Nγ (Hansen) = 1.5(Nq − 1) tanφ

Shape factors (Meyerhof):

Sc = 1 + (B/L)(Nq/Nc)
Sq = 1 + (B/L) tanφ
Sγ = 1 − 0.4 (B/L) ≥ 0.6

Depth factors (Meyerhof): For Df/B ≤ 1 use (Df/B); for > 1 use arctan(Df/B) in radians.

dc = 1 + 0.4 k
dq = 1 + 2 tanφ (1 − sinφ)² · k
dγ = 1

Load-inclination factors (Meyerhof):

ic = iq = (1 − α°/90°)²
iγ = (1 − α°/φ°)² ; 0 when α ≥ φ

Groundwater correction: If Dw < Df, the surcharge term q uses buoyant γ' for the submerged portion. If Dw < Df + B, the γ in the third term is reduced to γ' = γsat − γw (linearly interpolated in the transition). Otherwise full γ is used. Per Das, Principles of Foundation Engineering, 8th ed., Eqs. 4.14–4.15.

ACI 318 shear: φVc = 0.75 · 2√f'c · b · d (one-way, psi). For two-way: φVc = 0.75 · min(4√f'c, (2+4/β)√f'c, (2+αs d/b0)√f'c) · b0 · d.

About this tool

Free online footing bearing capacity calculator for civil, structural, and geotechnical engineers. Computes Terzaghi ultimate bearing capacity with Meyerhof / Vesic / Hansen Nγ, shape, depth, and inclination factors. Includes groundwater correction and ACI 318 one-way & two-way (punching) shear checks for reinforced-concrete spread footings.

Workflow:

  1. Pick a soil preset or enter custom c, φ, γ.
  2. Enter footing dimensions B × L, embedment Df, and effective depth d.
  3. Enter service load P (for bearing) and factored Pu (for shear).
  4. Adjust FS (default 3.0), toggle groundwater via Dw.
  5. Click Calculate — or Size B to auto-solve minimum width.
  6. Review PASS/FAIL verdicts on bearing, one-way shear, and two-way punching.
