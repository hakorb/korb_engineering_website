About This Tool

Manning's Open Channel Flow Calculator computes uniform (normal) flow through prismatic open channels using the Manning equation. It handles the five most common cross-section shapes and solves inversely for normal depth when given the discharge.

Equations

US Customary: Q = (1.486/n) · A · R2/3 · S1/2

SI (metric): Q = (1/n) · A · R2/3 · S1/2

Froude Number: Fr = V / √(g · Dh), where Dh = A/T

Critical Depth: Q²/g = A³/T (solved iteratively)

Boundary Shear Stress: τ = γ · R · S

Solution Methods

  • Forward (Q from y): direct computation
  • Inverse (y from Q): Newton-Raphson iteration on Manning’s equation
  • Critical depth: Newton-Raphson on the critical flow condition Q²T/(gA³) = 1
  • Tolerance: 1e-6; max 100 iterations

Assumptions & Limitations

  • Steady, uniform flow (normal depth)
  • Prismatic channel with constant cross-section
  • Turbulent flow, rough channel regime
  • Does not account for backwater, gradually varied flow, or energy losses at transitions
  • Parabolic section uses simplified approximations for wetted perimeter
  • Circular pipes: valid for 0 < y < D; near-full conditions approach asymptotic limits

References

  • Chow, V.T. (1959). Open-Channel Hydraulics. McGraw-Hill.
  • Chaudhry, M.H. (2008). Open-Channel Flow, 2nd Ed. Springer.
  • FHWA HDS-5 (2012). Hydraulic Design of Highway Culverts.

For complex geometries, transitions, inlet/outlet control, backwater profiles, or composite sections use the HEC-RAS Open tool, also available from Korb Engineering.
