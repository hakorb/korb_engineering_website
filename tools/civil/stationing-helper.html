ABOUT STATION NOTATION

A station is a measured distance along a linear alignment (highway centerline, pipeline, rail, runway, channel). In US practice one station = 100 feet; in metric practice one station = 1000 metres (1 km).

The notation 12+34.56 means 12 full stations plus 34.56 feet, i.e. 1234.56 ft from the point of beginning (POB). The + is the station separator, not an arithmetic operator. Metric notation 1+234.567 means 1234.567 m.

WHY IT'S USED

  • Field crews can call out stations without confusion between "feet" and "stations".
  • Plan sheets show a continuous chainage that plots cleanly on profile views.
  • Plus-station values always stay below 100 (or 1000 m) so errors are easy to spot.
  • Horizontal curves and vertical curves reference PC/PT/PVC/PVT stations directly.

KEY POINTS ON ALIGNMENTS

  • POB — Point of Beginning (start of alignment)
  • PI — Point of Intersection of two tangents
  • PC / PT — Point of Curvature / Point of Tangency (horizontal)
  • PVI / PVC / PVT — Vertical PI / Curve / Tangent
  • T, L, LC, E, M, D, R, Δ — Tangent, Length, Long Chord, External, Middle ordinate, Degree of curve, Radius, Deflection

FORMULA REFERENCE

  • T = R · tan(Δ/2)
  • L = R · Δ (arc definition, Δ in rad)
  • LC = 2R · sin(Δ/2)
  • E = R · (sec(Δ/2) − 1)
  • M = R · (1 − cos(Δ/2))
  • Darc = 5729.58 / R (degrees per 100 ft)
  • K = L / |g2 − g1| (vertical curve rate of vertical curvature)

DESIGN REFERENCES

  • AASHTO — A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets (Green Book). See Chapter 3 for vertical curve K-values, stopping sight distance (SSD), and passing sight distance criteria.
  • FAA Advisory Circular AC 150/5300-13B — Airport Design. Runway longitudinal grade limits (max 1.5% for Group III+, 2% for smaller aircraft), vertical curve length requirements.
  • FHWA — Highway Capacity Manual and Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices (MUTCD).
  • State DOT design manuals (TxDOT Roadway Design Manual, FDOT Design Manual, etc.) for superelevation, curve minimums, and runoff transitions.

NOTE

This tool performs geometric math only. It does not apply AASHTO sight-distance policy, superelevation transitions, or safety checks. Use it to speed up field calcs, quantity takeoffs, and sanity-checks on shop drawings. Always cross-verify against signed/sealed design plans.
