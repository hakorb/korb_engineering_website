🐒 The Horses

War Admiral — Winner of the 1937 Triple Crown (Kentucky Derby, Preakness Stakes, Belmont Stakes). Son of Man o' War and bred by Samuel Riddle at Glen Riddle Farm in Pennsylvania. War Admiral won 21 of 26 career starts and was known for his fierce competitive spirit despite his relatively small stature.
Man o' War — Considered one of the greatest thoroughbred racehorses of all time. Won 20 of 21 career races between 1919-1920. His only loss came at Sanford Memorial Stakes to a horse aptly named "Upset." Man o' War sired 64 stakes winners and his bloodline influenced racing for generations. Owned by Samuel Riddle, who named his Glen Riddle Farm after the town in Pennsylvania.

🏗 Course Architects

Joel Weiman & McDonald Design Group designed both the War Admiral and Man O'War courses. Weiman is known for creating strategic, aesthetically beautiful layouts that integrate naturally with their surroundings.
Jim Furyk served as Design Consultant on the War Admiral course. A PGA Tour legend from the Delmarva area, Furyk is known for his unique swing and consistency. He holds the record for the lowest round in PGA Tour history with a 58 at the 2016 Travelers Championship. His influence on War Admiral emphasizes strategic shot-making over raw power.

🌎 Berlin, Maryland

Named "America's Coolest Small Town" by Budget Travel magazine. Located on the Delmarva Peninsula, just minutes from Ocean City, MD. Berlin features a charming downtown with historic buildings, unique shops, and restaurants. The town's Main Street is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
