        Blackland Prairie Soil 101

        Frisco sits on heavy clay (Houston Black & Austin series) — dark, rich in minerals, but slow-draining, prone to cracking in summer, and compacts when wet. Good news: it holds nutrients well. Bad news: root rot is a real risk if you plant thirsty crops in low spots.

        Amendments That Actually Work

        • Expanded shale — the gold standard for breaking up clay. Work 3" into the top 8" of new beds. Permanent fix.
        • Compost — 2–3" annually, top-dressed or lightly tilled. Feeds soil biology.
        • Gypsum — helps flocculate clay. ~40 lb per 100 sq ft once, then every 2–3 years.
        • Avoid peat moss — acidic and short-lived in our alkaline clay. Skip it.
        • Avoid sand alone — clay + sand = concrete. Always pair with organic matter.

        pH Reality Check

        Blackland soils run pH 7.5–8.2. Iron chlorosis (yellow leaves with green veins) is common on azaleas, gardenias, pin oaks — skip those or grow in containers with acidic potting mix. Use chelated iron as a short-term fix.

        Watering the Right Way

        Deep & infrequent beats shallow & daily. Roots follow moisture — shallow watering makes shallow roots, which die in July.

        Established Landscape Rule of Thumb

        • Turf: 1" per week total (rain + irrigation). Split into 2 cycles.
        • Trees: slow soak at the drip line every 2–3 weeks in summer.
        • Shrubs: deep soak weekly May–September.
        • Perennials: let top 1" dry between waterings.
        • Veggies: consistent moisture, never soggy — drip or soaker hoses.

        Time of Day

        Water between 4 AM and 10 AM. Evening watering invites fungal disease (brown patch, take-all root rot). Avoid overhead watering of foliage in summer heat — it can scald leaves.

        Frisco Drought Rules

        Frisco typically runs a Stage 1–2 watering restriction. Outdoor watering is usually limited to 2 days per week, before 10 AM / after 6 PM, assigned by address. Hand watering and drip are usually exempt. Check friscotexas.gov for current stage.

        Rain Barrels

        A single 50-gal barrel off a 1000 sq ft roof captures ~600 gal/yr in Frisco (~36"/yr rainfall). Use for container plants and veggies — slightly acidic rainwater is a treat for chlorosis-prone plants.

        Mulch Like You Mean It

        3" of hardwood or native shredded mulch. Keep it 2" back from trunks (no volcanoes). Refresh each spring. In Frisco's heat, mulch cuts watering needs roughly in half and keeps clay from cracking.
