Strategy Guide & House Edge
▼
Best Bets (Low House Edge)
  • Don't Pass: 1.36% edge — Slightly better than Pass Line, but you're betting against the shooter (unpopular socially).
  • Pass Line: 1.41% edge — The classic bet. Win on 7/11 on come-out, establish a point and win if rolled before 7.
  • Free Odds (Behind Pass/Don't Pass): 0% edge — The best bet in the casino. Take odds behind your Pass Line bet for a true-odds payout.
  • Place 6 or 8: 1.52% edge — Solid if you want more action. Pays 7:6.
Sucker Bets to Avoid
  • Any Seven: 16.67% edge — Worst bet on the table. Pays 4:1 but true odds are 5:1.
  • Any Craps: 11.11% edge — Pays 7:1 on 2/3/12 rolling next.
  • Hardways (Hard 4, 10): 11.11% edge.
  • Hardways (Hard 6, 8): 9.09% edge — Must roll as a pair before either a soft number or a 7.
  • Big 6 / Big 8: 9.09% edge — Same as Place 6/8 but pays worse (1:1 vs 7:6). Avoid.
  • Field: ~2.78% edge — Tempting because 7 numbers win, but you lose on 5/6/7/8 (20 of 36 rolls).

Betting Progression Basics
  • Start with a single Pass Line bet. Keep it flat — don't chase losses.
  • When a point is set, place a Free Odds bet behind it (same amount or max odds if you can afford it).
  • Session bankroll rule: risk no more than 1-2% of your bankroll per round.
  • Walk away when up 20-30% or down to your stop-loss (typically 50% of buy-in).
  • Ignore "hot streak" bets — each roll is independent. The dice have no memory.
Round Flow Cheatsheet
  • Come Out Roll: Pass wins on 7 or 11. Loses on 2, 3, 12 (craps). Any other number (4/5/6/8/9/10) becomes the point.
  • Point Round: Shooter rolls until they roll the point again (Pass wins, seven-out loses) or a 7 (Pass loses, "seven-out").
  • Don't Pass: Mirror image. Loses on 7/11, wins on 2/3 (12 is push), then wins on 7 before the point.
  • Come Bet: Like a Pass bet but placed mid-round. The next roll becomes its "come-out."
