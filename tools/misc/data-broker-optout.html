Iterative checklist to scrub your data from 50+ people-search sites. Tracks status locally; no data leaves this browser.
// Read Me First

What is a data broker? Companies that scrape public records, social media, purchase histories, and leaked breaches into searchable dossiers about you — your home address, relatives, phone numbers, political affiliations, estimated income. They sell this to advertisers, debt collectors, background-check services, and anyone with a credit card.

Why opt out? Lower stalking/doxxing risk. Fewer robocalls and phishing lures. Reduce identity-theft attack surface. Make yourself harder to target with political microads. It is also simply your data — you did not consent to it being sold.

The hard truth: Most brokers will re-add your record within 6-12 months, pulled fresh from new public records. Opt-out is recurring maintenance, not a one-time fix. Budget an afternoon every 6 months, or pay a service like DeleteMe / Kanary / Optery to automate it.

Before you start:

  • Create a dedicated opt-out email (e.g. optout.yourname@gmail.com) — many brokers require confirmation and will spam it.
  • Use a VoIP number (Google Voice) if they require a phone. Never give your SSN. Never pay a broker to remove you — legitimate opt-outs are free.
  • Some brokers require a photo ID. You can redact everything except name + photo before uploading.
  • Start with the aggregators (LexisNexis, Acxiom) — they feed data to downstream sites.
  • This tool stores status only in your browser's localStorage. Clearing cookies/site-data wipes it. Use Export JSON to back up.

Legal note: California (CCPA/CPRA), Colorado, Virginia, Connecticut, Utah, Texas, Oregon, Montana, Delaware, Iowa, Tennessee, Indiana, Florida residents have statutory opt-out rights. EU/UK residents have GDPR/UK-GDPR rights. Even if you are not in those jurisdictions, most brokers process all requests uniformly — cite CCPA anyway.
