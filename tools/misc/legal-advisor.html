ⓘ How This Tool Works

What this tool is: A legal information reference that helps you understand general legal concepts, processes, and your rights in Texas, Oklahoma, and New Mexico. It cites specific statutes so you can verify information independently.

What this tool is NOT: This is not legal advice. It cannot analyze your specific situation, represent you, or create an attorney-client relationship. It is not a substitute for a licensed attorney.

Data sources: Information is compiled from official state statutes, legal codes, and publicly available legal resources. Statute citations reference real code sections. However, laws change and this tool may not reflect the most recent amendments.

Confidence indicators:

  • Well-Established — Fundamental legal principles unlikely to change significantly
  • Verify Current Law — Details that may have been amended; check current statute
  • Consult Attorney — Complex area with many exceptions; professional guidance recommended

Privacy: This tool stores NO data on any server. All notes, bookmarks, and preferences are saved in your browser's localStorage on your device only. No cookies, no tracking, no analytics, no data transmission. You can delete all data at any time.

Last content review: January 2025. Statutes cited were verified against official state code databases as of that date.
