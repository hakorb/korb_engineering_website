What is this?

A simulation of the classic 1970s vibrating-field electric football game (Tudor / Coleco style). A green metal field buzzes, and plastic players on bristly bases skitter across it — whichever direction the base is pointed. The ball carrier has to reach the opposite end zone without being touched by a defender. Half the fun was the unpredictable chaos when you flipped the switch.

Setup phase

Drag any player to move them before the snap. Tap a player (no drag) to cycle their base orientation 45°. The little arrow on each base shows the direction they'll scoot when the field vibrates. Your goal is to point blockers downfield and stack them against the defenders' path to your runner.

By default, HOME is offense (red) and AWAY is defense (navy). The yellow-ringed red player is the ball carrier — tap CHOOSE RUNNER and then click any offensive player to hand him the ball.

Running a play

Press ⚡ FLIP SWITCH. The field starts buzzing, every player skitters along their base direction (with random jitter), and collisions push them around like real vibrating figures. The play ends when:

  • A defender touches the ball carrier → tackled where he stands
  • The ball carrier crosses the far goal line → touchdown (7 pts)
  • The runner goes out of bounds → play ends at that spot
  • 15 seconds of real-world buzzing → play dies (everyone runs out of steam)

On PASS plays, press FLIP SWITCH then tap any spot on the field — the runner throws and an orange ball streaks to that target. A defender in the flight path can intercept or bat it down.

Rules of thumb

Four 2-minute quarters · first down on 10 yards · four downs · turnover on downs · field goals auto-attempted within ~55yd with distance-based accuracy · punts flip field position.
