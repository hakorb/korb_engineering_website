Food-cost target 28-32% is standard for sit-down restaurants; 25-28% for fast-casual; 20-25% for fine-dining. Food trucks often run 30-35%. Packaging is usually lumped into overhead.
About
All data lives in your browser's localStorage under korb_recipe_* keys — no cloud, no account. Unit conversions are baked in: 1 cup = 16 tbsp = 48 tsp = 240 mL; 1 lb = 16 oz = 453.59 g; 1 stick butter = 113 g = 8 tbsp, and so on. Density-based ingredients (flour, sugar, etc.) have built-in volume-to-weight conversions so you can buy by the bag and cook by the cup.

Menu Pricing Formula: Price = Cost / (Food-Cost Target %). At a 30% target, a dish costing $3 gets priced at $10. Profit per serving is price minus total cost.
